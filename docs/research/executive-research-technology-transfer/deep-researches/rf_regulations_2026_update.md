# Russian AI Regulations and 152-FZ Compliance Requirements: 2026 Update

**Research Date:** March 2026  
**Classification:** Executive Research - Technology Transfer  
**Jurisdiction:** Russian Federation

---

## Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the current Russian regulatory framework for artificial intelligence (AI) and personal data protection as of March 2026. Russia is transitioning from an industry-specific, experimental legal approach toward a more cohesive regulatory framework. Key developments include the draft federal AI law expected to take effect on September 1, 2027, and significant amendments to Federal Law No. 152-FZ on personal data that have strengthened data localization requirements. The regulatory landscape emphasizes technological sovereignty, domestic AI development, and compliance with Russian "traditional spiritual and moral values."

---

## 1. Current Regulatory Framework Summary

### 1.1 Personal Data Protection: Federal Law No. 152-FZ

The foundation of Russian data protection law remains Federal Law No. 152-FZ "On Personal Data" (dated July 27, 2006). This law establishes:

- **Scope:** Applies to any organization processing personal data of Russian citizens, regardless of where the organization is located
- **Data Categories Covered:**
  - Basic identifiers (name, address, phone, email, passport)
  - Biometric and special categories (gender, ethnicity, fingerprints, DNA)
  - Professional and financial information
  - Online and digital data (IP addresses, cookies, browsing history)

- **Core Principles:**
  - Lawful basis required for processing (consent, contract performance, legal obligation, etc.)
  - Data subject rights: access, rectification, deletion, objection to automated decision-making
  - Cross-border transfer restrictions

### 1.2 AI Regulation: Industry-Specific Approach

Russia currently regulates AI through an **industry-specific framework** rather than a comprehensive AI law. Key sector-specific guidelines include:

| Sector | Key Requirements |
|--------|-------------------|
| Healthcare | Clear accountability for AI in medical practice |
| Education | AI prohibited from replacing students in completing assignments |
| Transportation | Safety standards and operator responsibility |
| Agriculture | Compliance with agricultural regulations |
| Construction | Safety and quality standards |

### 1.3 Strategic AI Policy Documents

Russia has adopted several strategic documents guiding AI development:

1. **National AI Strategy** - Establishes development priorities
2. **AI Development Concept** - Updated framework effective 2025
3. **Regulatory Sandboxes (Experimental Legal Regimes)** - Since 2020, allows testing AI innovations under modified regulations
4. **Presidential Instructions (January 2026)** - Directs government to draft national AI implementation plan

## 2. Recent Changes (2025-2026)

### 2.1 Personal Data Law Amendments (Effective July 1, 2025)

**Federal Law No. 23-FZ (February 28, 2025)** amended Article 18(5) of 152-FZ:

| Aspect | Previous Requirement | New Requirement (Post-July 1, 2025) |
|--------|---------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Localization | Operators "shall ensure" recording using databases in Russia | "Shall not permit" use of foreign databases for Russian citizens |
| Scope | Databases must be in Russia | Complete prohibition on processing via foreign databases |
| Exceptions | Limited | Only clauses 2, 3, 4, and 8 of Article 6(1) apply |

**Key Change:** The language shifted from a positive obligation to a prohibition, making compliance more stringent.

### 2.2 Consent Requirements (Effective September 1, 2025)

**Federal Law No. 156-FZ (June 24, 2025)** amended Article 9(1) of 152-FZ:

- **Separation Requirement:** Consent must be obtained as a **separate document**, not embedded in contracts or user agreements
- **Applicability:** Applies to both written consent and consent "in any form"
- **Purpose:** Eliminate "hidden consents" and ensure informed, voluntary consent

### 2.3 Mandatory Anonymized Data Sharing (Effective September 1, 2025)

**Federal Law No. 233-FZ (August 8, 2024)** introduced Article 13.1:

- Operators must transfer anonymized personal data to the State Information System "Unified Information Platform of the National Data Management System"
- Purpose: Enable AI model training using aggregated datasets
- **Restrictions:** Data sets may not include biometric or special categories of personal data

### 2.4 Increased Penalties (Effective May 30, 2025)

**Federal Law No. 420-FZ (November 2024)** increased administrative fines:

- Maximum fines for violations increased to **18 million RUB**
- Non-compliance with localization requirements triggers minimum fines of 1 million RUB

### 2.5 Draft Federal AI Law (In Consultation, Expected September 1, 2027)

The Ministry of Digital Development published a draft "Law on Fundamentals of State Regulation of the Fields of Application of Artificial Intelligence Technologies" in March 2026 (consultation until April 15, 2026).

**Key Provisions:**

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Sovereign AI Models** | Must be fully created in Russia by Russian citizens/legal entities using Russian data sources |
| **National AI Models** | Similar to sovereign, with additional government registration |
| **Trusted AI Models** | Entered into a special government register |
| **Data Localization** | AI services with >500,000 daily users must store Russian user data in Russia for 3 years |
| **Content Labeling** | All AI-generated audio-visual materials must include warning labels |
| **Critical Industries** | Medicine, critical infrastructure, public administration: limited use of foreign models |
| **Discrimination Prevention** | Developers must eliminate discriminatory algorithms |
| **Liability** | Developers liable for harm caused by AI systems |
| **Deepfake Accountability** | Specific provisions for synthetic content |

**Expected Effective Date:** September 1, 2027

### 2.6 Foreign AI Tool Restrictions (Proposed)

Ministry for Digital Development proposals (March 2026):

- Foreign AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) may be banned or restricted if they fail to comply
- Cross-border AI technology may be prohibited if it "inevitably transfers data of Russian citizens abroad"
- AI must respect "traditional Russian spiritual and moral values"
- Rules expected to enter force in 2027 after government approval

## 3. Compliance Requirements for AI Projects

### 3.1 Data Localization Requirements

For any AI project involving Russian citizens' personal data:

1. **Database Location:** Primary databases must be located in Russia
2. **Processing Scope:** Recording, systematization, storage, retrieval must occur in Russia
3. **Cross-Border Exceptions:** Only under specific conditions (Article 6(1), clauses 2, 3, 4, 8)
4. **Mirror Databases:** May exist outside Russia only with original/master databases in Russia

### 3.2 AI-Specific Requirements (2027+)

For AI systems operating in Russia:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Documentation** | Document architecture, functional logic, and limitations of AI models |
| **Risk Assessment** | Assess potential risks associated with AI technologies |
| **Safe Operation Manual** | Include safe operation documentation |
| **Behavioral Prohibitions** | Prohibit use for manipulating behavior or exploiting human vulnerabilities |
| **Discrimination Prevention** | Ensure AI does not lead to discrimination based on behavior or personal characteristics |
| **Labeling** | Label all AI-generated content |
| **Human Control** | Maintain human oversight of AI systems |

### 3.3 Financial Sector Requirements

The Bank of Russia (Central Bank) has adopted a **risk-based, technology-neutral approach**:

- No special rules drafted yet for AI use by financial institutions
- 20% of financial market organizations already use AI; another 30% plan implementation within 3 years
- **Code of Ethics for AI in Finance** - Voluntary compliance framework
- Research focus 2026-2028: AI impact on economy, competition, financial stability

### 3.4 Consent Requirements

- Consent must be **separate from other documents**
- Must be **informed and voluntary**
- Data subjects have right to challenge AI-assisted government decisions through pre-trial procedure
- May seek compensation for AI-caused harm

## 4. Implications for Enterprise AI Projects

### 4.1 Key Considerations

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Data Residency** | Mandatory storage of Russian citizen data in Russia |
| **Vendor Selection** | Preference for domestic AI providers (Sberbank, Yandex) |
| **Cross-Border Transfers** | Significantly restricted; limited exceptions |
| **Content Generation** | Labeling requirements for AI-generated materials |
| **Compliance Costs** | Infrastructure investment required for data localization |
| **Penalties** | Fines up to 18 million RUB for violations |

### 4.2 Recommended Approach

1. **Data Mapping:** Identify all Russian personal data processed
2. **Infrastructure Review:** Ensure primary databases in Russia
3. **Vendor Due diligence:** Verify AI vendor compliance status
4. **Consent Management:** Implement separate consent mechanisms
5. **Monitoring:** Track regulatory developments, especially for 2027 AI law
