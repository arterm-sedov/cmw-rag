Influence of FinOps maturity  \n\n### 3.1 Maturity stages & capabilities  \n\nFinOps maturity follows a **Crawl → Walk → Run** progression (tagging, cost allocation, forecasting, automated optimization, chargeback) [10].  \n\n| Maturity level | Typical AI‑related practices | Impact on AI outcomes |\n|----------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------|\n| **Crawl** | Basic cost visibility, manual reporting, AI spend tracked by finance but not allocated. | Limited production; AI projects linger in pilot. |\n| **Walk** | Systematic allocation, forecasting, anomaly detection; AI cost management adoption rises to **63 %** (2025). | Faster move from pilot to production; early ROI visible. |\n| **Run** | Real‑time cost telemetry embedded in CI/CD, automated right‑sizing, AI‑for‑FinOps analytics; **98 %** of organizations now manage AI spend (2026). | **75 %** faster time‑to‑market, higher ROI, better governance. |\n\n### 3.2 Correlation with deployment & ROI  \n\n* **Visibility first:** 63 % of FinOps teams now focus on understanding AI spend (cost ingestion, allocation, forecasting) before optimization (FinOps 2025 report). Once visibility is achieved, organizations report **up to 75 %** faster AI time‑to‑market (Portkey).  \n* **Governance rise:** As maturity advances, governance and policy enforcement become top priorities, directly linking AI spend to business value (FinOps 2026 shift‑left).  \n* **Optimization later:** Optimization of GPU usage, token pricing, and model placement becomes a priority **only after** cost‑visibility and allocation are mature (FinOps 2025).  \n\n### 3.3 Size & industry differences  \n\n| Segment | FinOps maturity & AI spend handling | Production/ROI trends |\n|---------|--------------------------------------|-----------------------|\n| **Large enterprises (> $5 B revenue)** | Higher likelihood of “Run” maturity, dedicated AI‑FinOps domain, 98 % managing AI spend. | **≈ 50 %** of AI projects in production; stronger EBIT impact (McKinsey high‑performers). |\n| **Mid‑size firms** | Typically at “Walk” stage; AI cost visibility improving (63 % adoption). | **≈ 30‑35 %** production; ROI modest (cost‑savings > 20 %). |\n| **SMBs (< $100 M revenue)** | Predominantly “Crawl”; limited AI budgeting, higher reliance on PLG tools. | **≈ 20 %** production; ROI often limited to productivity gains. |\n| **Financial Services** | Heavier private‑cloud investment, AI spend management emphasized (FinOps 2025). | Higher cost‑reduction focus (10‑19 % supply‑chain savings). |\n| **Life Sciences** | Lead in AI maturity (IDC), more “transformative” stage projects. | Larger revenue uplift (6‑10 % reported). |\n| **Manufacturing, Retail, Telecom** | Mixed maturity; many at “Walk”. | Production rates around 30‑40 %; cost‑saving focus. |\n\nGeographically, the Deloitte survey (24 countries) shows **no major regional gaps** in the trend toward higher production rates; however, the Gartner I&O survey (US, UK, India, Germany) notes **54 %** of leaders adopt AI specifically to cut costs, reflecting regional cost‑pressure drivers [11].\n\n---\n\n## 4.
