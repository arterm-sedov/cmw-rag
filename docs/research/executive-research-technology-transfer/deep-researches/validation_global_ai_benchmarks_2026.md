## 1. McKinsey State of AI 2025/2026

**Source:** McKinsey "The State of AI: Global Survey 2025" (Published 2025)

### Key Findings

#### 1.1 AI Adoption Rate
- **88%** of organizations now use AI in at least one business function
- Up from 78% the previous year
- Nearly universal adoption among large enterprises ($5B+ revenue): **100%**

#### 1.2 The Scaling Gap
- Only **one-third (~33%)** of organizations have begun scaling AI across the enterprise
- **67% remain in pilot purgatory** — running experiments that never graduate to production
- Larger organizations ($5B+ revenue) are **~2× more likely to scale** (47% vs 29% for companies under $100M)

#### 1.3 AI Agents
- **62%** of organizations are already experimenting with AI agents
- **23%** are scaling at least one agent system
- Agent adoption is strongest in IT, knowledge management, and service operations
- High performers are **3× more likely** to be scaling agents

#### 1.4 High Performers (EBIT Impact)
- **6%** of respondents achieve >5% EBIT from AI — defined as "AI high performers"
- These organizations report:
  - **39%** see measurable EBIT contribution
  - **64%** report improved innovation
  - **45%** improved customer satisfaction
- Key differentiators: transformative ambition, workflow redesign, leadership alignment

#### 1.5 ROI and Value
- Generative AI value potential: **$2.6–$4.4 trillion** annually across 63 identified use cases
- High performers invest **>20% of digital budgets** in AI
- **80%** of organizations cite efficiency as top AI objective

#### 1.6 Workforce Impact
- Many companies have retrained **up to 10%** of the workforce due to AI
- Most organizations are **redeploying time saved** into new tasks rather than cutting headcount
- **32%** of leaders expect AI to reduce workforce size next year; **13%** expect increase

#### 1.7 Leadership Gap
- Only **14%** of organizations have leaders consistently championing AI adoption
- **1 in 6** companies have no C-level owner for AI

## 2. Deloitte State of AI 2026

**Source:** Deloitte "The State of AI in the Enterprise — 2026 AI Report" (Published March 2026)  
**Methodology:** 3,235 senior leaders surveyed, August–September 2025, 24 countries

### Key Findings

#### 2.1 Adoption vs Execution Gap
- **Access to AI tools jumped ~50%** in 2025 (from <40% to ~60% of employees)
- However, **fewer than 60%** of employees with access regularly use AI tools
- Only **25%** of organizations have converted **≥40%** of pilots into production systems

#### 2.2 Transformation vs Optimization
- **34%** of organizations are reimagining their business with AI (transformative)
- **66%** are just automating existing processes (optimization only)
- Deloitte calls this "the untapped edge"

#### 2.3 Production Deployment
- **25%** already have ≥40% of AI experiments in production
- **54%** expect to reach that level within **3–6 months**

#### 2.4 Investment and Returns
- **84%** are increasing AI investments
- Only **20%** see clear revenue impact so far

#### 2.5 Agentic AI
- **74%** plan to deploy agentic AI within **2 years**
- Governance maturity still lags

#### 2.6 Physical AI
- **57%** of Australian organizations already deploying physical AI (robots, automated machinery)
- Expected to exceed **80%** within two years globally

#### 2.7 Sovereign AI
- **72%** of companies now consider country of origin in vendor decisions
- **>80%** see it as a priority in strategic planning
- **77%** say where AI is developed matters

#### 2.8 Productivity Benefits
- **66%** of organizations report productivity and efficiency gains from enterprise AI adoption

## 3. Menlo Ventures — Generative AI 2025/2026

**Source:** Menlo Ventures "2025: The State of Generative AI in the Enterprise" (December 2025)

### Key Findings

#### 3.1 Market Size
- **$37 billion** total enterprise spending on generative AI in 2025
- **3.2× increase** year-over-year (from $11.5B in 2024)
- Fastest-scaling software category in history

#### 3.2 Application Layer ($19B)
- **Horizontal AI (Copilots):** $8.4B (5.3× YoY growth)
  - AI assistants: $7.2B (86% of horizontal)
  - Agent platforms: $750M (10%)
  - Personal productivity: $450M (5%)
- **Departmental AI:** $7.3B (4.1× YoY)
  - Coding: $4.0B (55% — largest category)
  - IT: 10%, Marketing: 9%, Customer Success: 9%, Design: 7%, HR: 5%
- **Vertical AI:** $3.5B (nearly 3× from 2024)
  - Healthcare: $1.5B (43% of vertical)
  - Legal: $650M, Creator tools: $360M, Government: $350M

#### 3.3 Infrastructure Layer ($18B)
- Up **2.0×** from $9.2B in 2024

#### 3.4 Market Share (Enterprise)
- **Anthropic (Claude):** 40% market share (up from 24%)
- **OpenAI:** 27% (down from 50% two years ago)
- **In coding:** Claude dominates with **54%** market share

#### 3.5 Healthcare AI
- Healthcare AI spending: **$1.4B** (nearly tripling 2024)
- Healthcare deploying AI at **2.2×** the rate of the broader economy
- Startups capture **85%** of healthcare AI spend

#### 3.6 Consumer AI
- Nearly **2 billion** people use AI globally
- Consumer AI market: **$12 billion**
- Only **~3%** conversion rate to paid premium services

## 4. Stanford AI Index 2025/2026

**Source:** Stanford HAI "Artificial Intelligence Index Report 2025" (Published April 2025)  
**Citation:** Nestor Maslej et al., "The AI Index 2025 Annual Report," Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI, April 2025

### Key Findings

#### 4.1 AI Incidents (Security)
- **56.4% increase** in AI-related incidents year-over-year
- **233 reported cases** in 2024
- Incidents span data breaches, algorithmic failures, privacy violations
- Less than **two-thirds** of organizations actively mitigating known AI risks

#### 4.2 Private Investment
- **$150.79 billion** in private investment in AI startups in 2024
- Surpassed previous record of $120B+ in 2021

#### 4.3 Model Performance
- AI systems achieving human-level performance on increasingly complex benchmarks
- Inference costs dropped **280×** between November 2022 and October 2024
- Open-weight models closing gap with closed models (performance difference: 8% → 1.7%)

#### 4.4 Geographic Distribution
- **U.S.:** 40 notable AI models (lead)
- **China:** 15 models
- **Europe:** 3 models
- China leading in AI publications and patents

#### 4.5 Hallucination Rates (2025-2026 Updates)
- General hallucination rates: **3–20%** or higher on open factual benchmarks
- Reasoning models show mixed results: o3 series **33–51%** hallucination on PersonQA
- Medical AI: **23%** hallucination rate (best), nearly 1 in 4 responses contains fabricated information
- Legal AI: Stanford RegLab/HAI study shows legal models hallucinate in **1 out of 6+** queries

#### 4.6 Public Trust
- Public trust in AI companies: **50% → 47%** (declining)
- Regulatory activity in U.S. more than **doubled**

## 5. Productivity Perception vs Reality

### Key Studies

#### 5.1 METR Study (July 2025)
**16 experienced developers, 246 real-world tasks**

| Metric | Perception | Measured Reality |
|--------|------------|------------------|
| Expected speedup (pre-use) | +24% | — |
| Perceived speedup (post-use) | +20% | — |
| **Actual measured** | — | **-19%** |

**Perception Gap:** ~43 percentage points

> "When asked to estimate how much using AI would speed me up, I guessed around 24%. After actually using AI, I thought I'd been working about 20% faster. But objective measurements showed I was 19% slower."
> — Domenic Denicola, METR study participant

#### 5.2 Nielsen Norman Group (NN/G) Studies
- **66% average productivity gain** across three business domains
- Studies measured actual throughput, not self-reported
- Gains highest in cognitively demanding tasks (coding > writing > support)

#### 5.3 Harvard Business School
- **758 consultants** studied using AI
- Tasks within AI capabilities: **+40%** output quality
- Tasks outside AI capabilities: **-19 percentage points** less likely to get correct answer

#### 5.4 Stack Overflow Developer Survey
- **84%** adoption of AI coding tools
- Only **16.3%** report "great productivity gains"

#### 5.5 ActivTrak Research (2025-2026)
- Only **3%** of employees use AI at "peak productivity intensity"
- **57%** spend less than 1% of time in AI
- "AI Measurement Gap" between adoption and actual business impact
